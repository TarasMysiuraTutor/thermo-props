## Thermophysical properties of water

Water (H₂O) is one of the most important working fluids in thermal
engineering, power systems, and heat and mass transfer applications.
Its thermophysical properties strongly depend on temperature, pressure,
and phase state (ice, liquid, or vapor).

This page presents engineering-level calculations of water properties
based on approximation correlations consistent with the IAPWS framework.
The focus is on transparent physical assumptions and reliable results
within practical operating ranges rather than full tabular accuracy.

### Application range
- Temperature: −100 … 800 °C  
- Pressure: 0.001 … 100 MPa  

### Calculated properties
- Density  
- Specific heat capacity  
- Thermal conductivity  
- Dynamic and kinematic viscosity  
- Enthalpy  
- Phase behavior
